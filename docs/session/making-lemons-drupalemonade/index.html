Jared King is the principal and owner of Yeego Connect LLC, a 100 percent Native American-owned small business consulting firm specializing in cultural tourism and education, meetings and event production, travel management, communications, and public affairs. Yeego (pronounced YAY-Go) in Navajo means "Go for it!"

Drawing on his 15 years of experience as communications director for the Navajo Nation's Washington DC office and with the external affairs team at the Oakland, California-based Native American Health Center, Jared added consulting services to Yeego in January 2020, offering his wealth of expertise in public, government, and communications.

How we leveraged the pandemic to diversify our business and expand and upgrade our online presence

Yeego Connect LLC used the National Congress of American Indians/Google Small Business Stabilization Grant to redevelop our business model during the COVID-19 pandemic. We were a thriving full-service travel, meetings, and events small business before the pandemic and enjoyed double-digit year-over-year end growth since our inception in 2015. However, when the pandemic hit, business and leisure travel and in-person meetings and events came to a complete standstill worldwide.

This presentation will highlight how we used the grant to expand our business significantly into technical assistance and expertise for our clients in strategic planning and facilitation, meetings and events, travel, communications, web development, and graphic design. That work included a new brand identity, content re-architecture, and an upgrade from Drupal 7 to Drupal 9. We will discuss some of the planning and technical work involved, including the general site architecture, responsive design using Emulsify, and peek into the content migration effort.

This presentation will focus on strategy and implementation and should be accessible to those with an intermediate level of experience or greater.
